THE KILLER: Dr. Emil Kurtz
THE WEAPON: The Count's stiletto letter opener
THE MOTIVE: Revenge for a ruined reputation and career.

Dr. Kurtz, your composure has been your shield, but your story has unraveled. You were not simply the Count's physician; you were complicit in his schemes, and ultimately his victim.

Here is what happened: You were once a respected physician, but your reputation was tarnished. Perhaps it was a medical error, or perhaps you fell into debt. The Count, a man who preys on weakness, discovered your secret and used it to blackmail you. He "hired" you as his personal physician, but your real job was to use your medical knowledge to his advantage—perhaps by declaring a business rival unfit or by providing him with substances to control others. This is why you were on the train.

This arrangement soured. The scrap of paper found under the Count's body reading, "...cannot forgive what you have done to..." was not from Teodora Novak; it was from you. The Count had ruined you, and you could no longer bear it.

On the night of the murder, you saw your chance. You knew the Count's habits. You knew he'd have a nightcap. Your initial alibi of being in your room reading was a lie, exposed by Major Hessler who saw you in the corridor. You then amended your story to a "stroll," and then further amended it to "checking the connecting door." This door was key. It was unbolted from the Car A side, where your cabin was. You unbolted it to create confusion and cast suspicion on the passengers in Car B.

You carry a leather medical bag. The polish on that bag matches the smear found on the inside of the Count's door handle. You were wearing gloves.

You went to the Count's cabin, knowing he would let his "doctor" in for a late-night consultation. The door was unlatched, not locked, because he was expecting you. You shared a glass of brandy with him, lulling him into a false sense of security. Then, when he was lying in his berth, you took the stiletto letter opener from his desk—a weapon of opportunity—and, with your precise medical knowledge, stabbed him once through the heart, ensuring instant death. You wiped the weapon and the glass clean, left the door unlatched, and slipped back to your adjacent cabin, A2, hoping the thin walls would be your alibi—who would suspect the man next door who heard nothing?

But you were seen. Your alibi crumbled. And your motive, though hidden, was the most powerful of all: the desperation of a man who had lost everything.

THE TRUTH REVEALED:

The killer was Teodora Novak (Young Serbian heiress traveling to Istanbul).

WEAPON: A stiletto letter opener taken from the Count's own writing desk in the compartment
MOTIVE: The Count had secretly been selling forged provenance documents with stolen artwork to Teodora's late father, bankrupting the family and driving her father to suicide. Teodora discovered the proof among her father's papers three months ago and boarded this train specifically to confront him — but when the Count laughed in her face during a private meeting in his cabin after dinner, she snapped.

WHAT REALLY HAPPENED:
After the champagne dinner ended around 11:30 PM, Teodora requested a private word with the Count in his cabin about 'a business matter regarding her father's estate.' The Count admitted her at 12:40 AM. During their conversation, she confronted him with the forged documents. He laughed and told her the proof would never hold up. In a rage, she grabbed the ornate stiletto letter opener from his writing desk and stabbed him. She wiped the handle with her glove, slipped out into the corridor at approximately 12:50 AM, and returned to her own cabin in Sleeping Car B. The body wasn't discovered until the porter tried to deliver morning coffee at 1:15 AM and found the door unlatched.

TIMELINE:
11:30 PM - Dinner ends, guests disperse. 12:15 AM - Most passengers retired. 12:40 AM - Teodora enters Count's cabin A4. 12:45 AM - Confrontation escalates. 12:48 AM - The Count is stabbed. 12:50 AM - Teodora exits, returns to Sleeping Car B. 1:15 AM - Porter discovers body, door unlatched, raises alarm.
