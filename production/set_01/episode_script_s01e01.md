### Exchange 1: Volkov (Round 1)

**NARRATOR:**
> The detective begins with the man closest to the victim's routine — the Count's personal valet.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Pyotr Volkov. Describe exactly what you did to prepare the Count's cabin for the evening, and when was the last time you saw him alive?

**VOLKOV (Llama):**
> I prepared the Count's cabin as I always do — turning down the bed, setting out his nightclothes, placing the brandy decanter on the side table. This was at 11:15, just as the dinner party was winding down. The last time I saw him alive, he was in his cabin, still in his dinner attire. He dismissed me with a nod and said he would retire shortly.

---

### Exchange 2: Marguerite (Round 1)

**NARRATOR:**
> Next — the woman who knew the Count most intimately.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Marguerite Fontaine. You were the Count's guest of honor at dinner. What was his mood during the evening, and when did you last see him? Did he mention planning to meet with anyone?

**MARGUERITE (DeepSeek):**
> He was in high spirits throughout dinner, celebrating his latest business triumph. I last saw him around 11:45 when I returned his cigarette case to his cabin. He seemed perfectly fine and mentioned nothing about meeting anyone. Though I did notice he had poured two brandy glasses — which struck me as odd, since he hadn't mentioned expecting visitors.

---

### Exchange 3: Hessler (Round 1)

**NARRATOR:**
> The detective turns to the man with the most public grudge.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Major Hessler. Your comment about the Count "pushing someone too far" was overheard at dinner. Where were you between 11:45 and 1:15, and did you have any private interaction with the Count afterward?

**HESSLER (ChatGPT):**
> I spent that time in the observation lounge, enjoying brandy and cigars. The barman can vouch for my presence until around 12:30. I had no private interaction with the Count after dinner. Our rivalry was well-known, but I had no intention of confronting him that evening.

---

### Exchange 4: Dr. Kurtz (Round 1)

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Dr. Kurtz — as the Count's attending physician, did you have any scheduled checks planned for him that night? And do you carry medical implements that could match the murder weapon?

**KURTZ (Gemini):**
> No, there were no scheduled treatments. He was to summon me only if he experienced distress. As for my implements — my medical bag contains scalpels, which are of course narrow blades. But they are instruments for healing, not for this kind of butchery.

---

### Exchange 5: Brandt (Round 1)

**NARRATOR:**
> The detective questions the only other trained observer on the train.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Felix Brandt. As someone hired to observe the Count — what notable interactions did you witness during or after dinner, and where were you between 11:45 and 1:15?

**BRANDT (Qwen):**
> I left dinner around 11:15, took a stroll through the train to familiarize myself with the layout, and returned to my cabin by midnight. I spent the time reading until the alarm went off. As for observations — the Count's interactions were strained, particularly with Hessler, whose animosity was palpable. Marguerite seemed to play her part well, but I couldn't shake the feeling she was more than just a kept woman. And the young Serbian heiress — Novak — kept to herself, which was curious in such a social setting.

---

### Exchange 6: Teodora (Round 1)

**NARRATOR:**
> And finally — the one who said the least at dinner.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Teodora Novak. You were notably reserved at dinner. What was the nature of your interaction with the Count, and where were you between 11:45 and 1:15?

**TEODORA (Mistral):**
> Oh, the Count and I barely exchanged words — just the usual pleasantries. As for my whereabouts, I retired to my cabin immediately after dinner, around 11:30. I read for a while, took a sleeping powder, and was quite drowsy by midnight. I didn't leave my compartment until the alarm was raised. My neighbor in B4 might confirm hearing me moving about until then.

---

### Exchange 7: Volkov (Round 2)

**NARRATOR:**
> The first round complete, the detective circles back — zeroing in on a detail that doesn't add up.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Volkov — when you prepared the Count's cabin at 11:15, did you set out one brandy glass or two? And did the Count typically receive visitors at that hour?

**VOLKOV (Llama):**
> I set out only one glass, as was the custom. It's unusual for him to receive visitors that late, unless it was someone he was... expecting. But I wasn't aware of any such appointments. The presence of a second glass, as Marguerite mentioned, suggests the Count anticipated a visitor after I had left.

---

### Exchange 8: Marguerite (Round 2)

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Marguerite — you mentioned returning the cigarette case at 11:45. Did you notice anything unusual about the Count's demeanor, and did you see anyone else in the corridor?

**MARGUERITE (DeepSeek):**
> He seemed composed, though perhaps a touch impatient — he barely glanced at the cigarette case before setting it aside. The corridor was empty when I passed through, though I did hear faint footsteps moving away toward the dining car as I left his cabin. And that second brandy glass was already poured when I arrived. I assumed he was expecting someone, but he didn't say who.

---

### Exchange 9: Dr. Kurtz (Round 2)

**NARRATOR:**
> A detail about the leather polish draws the detective back to the doctor.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Dr. Kurtz — you mentioned your bag contains scalpels. Do you also use leather polish on your medical bag? And where exactly was the bag during the evening?

**KURTZ (Gemini):**
> My medical bag is indeed made of leather and requires periodic maintenance with polish. The bag remained in my cabin, A2, for the entirety of the evening.

---

### Exchange 10: Hessler (Round 2)

**HESSLER (ChatGPT):**

---

### Exchange 11: Brandt (Round 2)

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**

**BRANDT (Qwen):**

### Exchange 12: Dr. Kurtz (Round 3)

**KURTZ (Gemini):**

### Exchange 13: Teodora (Round 2)

**NARRATOR:**
> The detective begins to probe deeper — testing the edges of each story.

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Teodora — what kind of sleeping powder was it? Did Dr. Kurtz prescribe it? And did you have any reason to know the Count before this journey?

**TEODORA (Mistral):**
> Oh, it was just a mild powder I picked up in Vienna — nothing prescribed. As for the Count, I'd never met him before. My father's business was shipping, not arms, so our circles never crossed. I suppose I might have seen his name in the papers, but that's all. *pauses* Though I suppose it's no secret my father's reputation suffered in his final months.

---

### Exchange 14: Marguerite (Round 3)

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**
> Marguerite — when you returned the cigarette case at 11:45, did you notice the Count's letter opener on his writing desk? And the footsteps you heard — were they quick or slow?

**MARGUERITE (DeepSeek):**
> The letter opener... yes, it was on his desk. I distinctly remember the ornate handle gleaming in the lamplight. As for the footsteps — they moved briskly but not hurriedly. More like someone trying to be discreet than someone in a panic. Though honestly, footsteps in a train corridor are hardly unusual at that hour.

---

### Exchange 15: Hessler (Round 3)

**DETECTIVE (Claude):**

**HESSLER (ChatGPT):**

I do know the Count had many disagreements with various people, and he received several letters during the journey. I didn't think much of it at the time. He was a man who inspired strong feelings. But now that you mention it — I do recall a letter with a Serbian stamp on the envelope. I didn't think to mention it earlier. It seemed like just another piece of his business.
